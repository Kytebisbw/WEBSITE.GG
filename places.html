Here are the top 10 Tourist spot in the Zamboanga City

  1. Peakview Café

    2. Situated a top a pictures que hill in Zamboanga City, Peakview Cafe offers more than just delightful cuisine—it provides an unforgettable dining experience with its stunning panoramic views.

    This is located at located at Km.10 Sta. Rita, La Paz Zambo Ecozone Highlands.


  2. Fort Pilar Shrine

    3. The Real Fuerte de Nuestra Señora del Pilar de Zaragoza, also Fort Pilar, is a 17th-century military defense fortress built by the Spanish colonial government in Zamboanga City. The fort, which is now a regional museum of the National Museum of the Philippines, is a major landmark of the city and it symbolize the cultural heritage. Outside the eastern wall is a Marian shrine dedicated to Our Lady of the Pillar, the patroness of the city, pontifically crowned on 12 October 1960 via decree dating from 18 September 1960.

    This is located at Valderosa St. Sta Barbara, Zamboanga City 7000.


  3. Paseo Del Mar


    4. Paseo Del Mar is located very close to the historical landmark and National Museum known as Fort Pilar. Paseo Del Mar is also known for its several restaurants, cafés and entertainment as well.

    This is located at right beside Fort Pilar, N S Valderosa St, Zamboanga City.


  4. Grande Santa Cruz Island

    5. Great Santa Cruz Island is a small inhabited island in Zamboanga City in the southern region of the Philippines that is famous for its pink coralline sand.

    The island, located 4 kilometres south of downtown at the Santa Cruz Bank in the Basilan Strait, boasts one of the pink sand beaches in the Philippines


  5. Merloquet Falls

    6. It is a two-tiered drop but its main attraction is the beautiful curtain waterfalls on its base, approximately 10-meter high and 15-meter wide stair-like wall to a shallow pool, definitely one-of-a-kind in the Philippines. The upper tier is a shorter drop and sloped diagonally, approximately 5-meter high and 10-meter wide.

    This is located at Brgy. Sibulao, Zamboanga Sibugay.


  6. Pasonanca Park

    7. The park consists of floral and butterfly species such as orchids, roses and colorful butterflies surrounding the garden. It also has an aviary park for the avian species such as parrots, turkeys, and eagles among the birds.

    You can find it at Brgy. Pasonanca, Zamboanga City


  7. R.T. Lim Boulevard

    8. The Boulevard of Zamboanga City is a picturesque waterfront promenade stretching along the coastline of the city. Lined with palm trees, benches, and vibrant gardens, it offers a scenic escape for locals and visitors alike. The boulevard is a popular spot for leisurely strolls, jogging, or simply enjoying the cool sea breeze while admiring the view of the bay. It's also dotted with food stalls and vendors offering local delicacies, making it a hub of activity and a favorite gathering place for residents. Whether you're looking to unwind amidst nature or savor the local flavors, the Boulevard of Zamboanga City provides a charming setting to relax and rejuvenate.


  8. Rainbow Masjid

    9. The Rainbow Masjid, located in Zamboanga City, is a striking symbol of unity and cultural diversity in the region. Its unique architecture features vibrant colors, including shades of red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple, hence its name "Rainbow Masjid." This mosque serves as a place of worship for the Muslim community in Zamboanga and stands as a testament to the city's rich Islamic heritage. Beyond its religious significance, the Rainbow Masjid has become a notable landmark, attracting tourists and visitors who admire its colorful facade and architectural beauty. It symbolizes the harmonious coexistence of different cultures and religions in Zamboanga City, making it a beacon of inclusivity and tolerance. The Rainbow Masjid, also known as the Masjid Al-Dahab Wal-Irida, is located in Zamboanga City, specifically in Barangay Talon-Talon. It's a distinctive landmark in the city due to its colorful architecture and serves as a significant place of worship for the Muslim community in Zamboanga.


  9. Museum

    10. The Zamboanga City Museum is a cultural institution dedicated to preserving and showcasing the history, heritage, and diverse cultural traditions of Zamboanga City and its surrounding areas. Located in the heart of the city, the museum offers visitors a glimpse into the rich tapestry of Zamboanga's past and present. Inside the museum, visitors can explore various exhibits featuring artifacts, artworks, photographs, and documents that chronicle the city's history, from its indigenous roots to its colonial past and contemporary culture. Exhibits may include displays on the indigenous peoples of the region, the Spanish colonial period, traditional crafts and arts, as well as contemporary cultural expressions. The museum also serves as an educational resource, hosting workshops, lectures, and events aimed at promoting awareness and appreciation of Zamboanga's cultural heritage. Through its exhibits and programs, the Zamboanga City Museum plays a vital role in preserving the city's identity and fostering pride among its residents while also welcoming visitors to learn and explore.


  10. Sadik Grand Mosque

    11. The Sadik Grand Mosque is a grand and historically significant Islamic mosque situated in Zamboanga City, Mindanao, Philippines. Named after Sheikh Karimul Makhdum, a revered Muslim missionary who introduced Islam to the Philippines in the 14th century, the mosque stands as one of the largest and most prominent mosques in the city and the region. It serves as a center for Islamic worship, community gatherings, and cultural events, attracting both local Muslims and visitors who come to admire its architectural beauty and learn about the Islamic heritage of Zamboanga City.
